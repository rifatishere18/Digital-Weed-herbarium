Chechra

Local name: চেঁচড়া

English name: Bog bulrush, Ricefield bulrush

Scientific name: Scirpus mucronatus

Family: Cyperaceae

Habit

Annual, erect, grass-like sedge, growing up to 50 cm tall.

Habitat

Found in wetlands, paddy fields, ditches, ponds, and other moist or waterlogged areas.

Growing season

Thrives during the monsoon and rainy seasons, predominantly in tropical and subtropical regions.

Associated crops

Commonly associated with rice fields; can also occur in other irrigated crops.

Propagating organ

Propagates through seeds and sometimes via rhizomes.

Leaf

Narrow, linear leaves that are often reduced, sheathing the base of the stem.

Stem

Triangular or cylindrical in shape, hollow, and green.

Inflorescence

Terminal clusters of small, brownish spikelets often with bracts at the base.

Root

Fibrous roots, sometimes with short rhizomes.

Control measures

Proper water management to prevent waterlogging. Manual weeding and tilling to remove young plants. Use of pre-emergent and post-emergent herbicides like bentazon or 2,4-D under expert guidance. Combine water management, crop rotation, and timely weeding for effective control.
