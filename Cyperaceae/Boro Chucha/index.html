Boro Chucha

Local name: বড় চুঁচা

English name: Rice flat sedge

Scientific name: Cyperus iria

Family: Cyperaceae

Habit

Annual sedge with a tufted growth form, growing up to 60 cm tall.

Habitat

Found in wet, marshy areas such as rice fields, irrigation canals, and waterlogged soils.

Growing season

Thrives during the rainy season, particularly in tropical and subtropical climates.

Associated crops

Commonly associated with rice, but also grows in fields of maize, sugarcane, and vegetables.

Propagating organ

Propagates primarily through seeds.

Leaf

Narrow, linear leaves that are bright green and arranged alternately.

Stem

Erect, triangular in cross-section, and smooth.

Inflorescence

Umbel-like clusters with multiple spikelets, yellowish-green to brown in color.

Root

Fibrous root system anchoring in wet soils.

Control measures

Proper water management and maintaining optimum crop spacing to reduce weed growth. Manual removal or tillage before seed maturation. Application of pre-emergence or post-emergence herbicides like butachlor or bensulfuron-methyl, specifically for rice fields.
