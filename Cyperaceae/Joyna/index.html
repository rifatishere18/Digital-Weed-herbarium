Joyna

Local name: জয়না

English name: Joyna, Grasslike fimbry, Hoorahgrass

Scientific name: Fimbristylis miliacea

Family: Cyperaceae

Habit

An annual sedge with slender stems, usually 10–50 cm tall, tufted and grass-like in appearance.

Habitat

Commonly found in wet, marshy areas, paddy fields, ditches, and lowlands; thrives in waterlogged conditions.

Growing season

Primarily grows during the rainy season or in irrigated fields.

Associated crops

Frequently associated with rice and other wetland crops.

Propagating organ

Reproduces through seeds and sometimes vegetatively from rootstocks.

Leaf

Linear, narrow, flat, or slightly folded, 10–20 cm long with smooth margins.

Stem

Erect, slender, triangular, and smooth.

Inflorescence

Umbel-like clusters of spikelets, usually brownish or reddish-brown, located at the tips of stems.

Root

Fibrous root system.

Control measures

Proper water management, hand weeding during the early stages of crop growth. Application of pre-emergence and post-emergence herbicides like butachlor or cyhalofop-butyl. Combining manual weeding with periodic herbicide use and ensuring proper field drainage to suppress growth.
